# Welcome to the Taste Economy

*By Jesse Dan-Yusuf.*

---

There was an attention economy. Then a trust economy. Then a creator economy.

All of them are now crowded, noisy, and exhausted.

Welcome to the Taste Economy.

There's a shift happening that most people are missing.

For the last twenty years, the creative economy rewarded speed. The designer who could crank out three concepts by Friday. The agency that could deliver a full brand in six weeks. The freelancer with the fastest turnaround. Speed was the game, and execution was the product.

That game is over.

AI can now generate a logo in three seconds, a full website in fifteen minutes, social content for a month in the time it takes you to brief a designer. The execution economy has been commoditized. If your value is tied to speed or output volume, you're competing with a machine that doesn't sleep.

What AI can't do is tell you which of those three logos actually means something. It can't explain why one feels premium and another feels cheap. It can't look at a brand and know what's missing before the founder even asks. It can't read a room, a culture, or a moment and adjust accordingly.

That's taste, and taste is the new economy.

When I talk about taste, I mean discernment that goes beyond aesthetic preference. The ability to see what works and what doesn't, and more importantly, why. The judgment to say "this, not that" and be right about it. The conviction to hold a line when a client wants to add one more thing that will ruin everything.

The new hierarchy of value

In the Taste Economy, value flows upward to those who can:

* Recognize what's worth keeping
* Reject what's merely competent
* Risk being wrong by being specific

This separates a creative director from a designer, a strategist from a prompter, a visionary from someone with a Canva Pro account.

In the taste economy, value comes from making better, from knowing what to keep and what to kill. From seeing the difference between decoration and direction. From understanding that curation, not generation, is the skill that matters.

Taste isn't a gift you're born with. It's a discipline you build through thousands of hours of looking, making, breaking, and refining. It's cultural intelligence, knowing how a Nigerian audience reads a brand versus a European one. It's the ability to see five variations of the same idea and know which one will land.

This is why FYREWORKS exists. Not to make things faster, but to make them right. To help visionaries build brands that don't just look good but work. Brands that mean something. Brands that move people.

In a world where anyone can generate anything, the question is no longer "can you make it?" but "should you?" Answering that question correctly, consistently, with conviction is what we do.

Welcome to the taste economy. The machines can execute. We focus on what they can't: direction, discernment, and judgment that matters.

—Jesse Dan-Yusuf
